# Audio Production Plan

Version date: April 14, 2026

This plan covers sound, music, haptics, and audio readability for the rebooted game.

## Audio role in the reboot

Audio should not be decorative only. It should help the game feel faster, clearer, and more satisfying.

The reboot needs audio to support:

- combat readability
- hit satisfaction
- danger anticipation
- reward cadence
- menu confidence
- mood and world identity

## Audio principles

- important information must never be audio-only
- sound should reinforce on-screen clarity, not replace it
- combat feedback should be punchy and readable even on phone speakers
- the mix must survive low-volume and noisy real-world play
- accessibility controls must remain real and easy to find

## Audio layers

### 1. UI and navigation

Needed for:

- button confirm
- back / cancel
- invalid input
- screen arrival
- reward reveal
- settings changes

Goal:

- fast confirmation with minimal annoyance

### 2. Combat readability

Needed for:

- player attack
- dodge
- hit taken
- enemy telegraph
- enemy hit confirmation
- enemy defeat
- pickup collection
- status applied / status cleared
- boss phase punctuation

Goal:

- the player should feel impact and timing without needing loud or cluttered effects

### 3. Reward and progression feedback

Needed for:

- upgrade offered
- upgrade locked in
- run clear
- run failure
- archive carry-forward
- unlock earned

Goal:

- rewards should feel earned and legible

### 4. Music and ambience

Needed for:

- title mood
- onboarding tension
- normal floor pulse
- elite / boss escalation
- post-run decompression

Goal:

- reinforce the corporate-horror tone without overpowering gameplay readability

## Recommended production path

### Phase 1: prototype audio set

For the first slice, build a minimal but functional audio kit:

- 6 to 10 UI sounds
- 8 to 12 combat sounds
- 3 short reward/progression stings
- 1 title loop
- 1 floor loop
- 1 boss loop

These can be created with a mix of:

- licensed sound libraries
- light synthesis
- edited stock textures
- AI-assisted ideation for references only

### Phase 2: slice polish

Refine the slice audio around:

- hit readability
- dodge clarity
- reward excitement
- boss pressure
- mix separation on phone speakers

### Phase 3: final production

For final shipping quality, strongly consider a professional sound designer or audio designer to:

- unify the sound palette
- build a consistent combat feedback language
- tune the mix for mobile speakers
- polish transitions
- create a distinct musical identity

## What AI can help with

AI can help with:

- reference generation
- mood exploration
- vocabulary for sound direction
- early music and ambience ideation

AI should not be the only final source for:

- all final combat feedback
- cohesive music identity
- polished mobile mix decisions
- reliable production-ready implementation choices

## Haptics

Haptics should remain optional and player-controlled.

Recommended haptic layers:

- light tap for UI confirm
- stronger pulse for player hit taken
- distinct pulse for reward claim
- short celebratory pulse for clear / unlock

Rule:

- haptics should reinforce impact, not create fatigue

## Accessibility requirements

- keep master and per-channel volume controls
- keep haptics toggle
- keep reduced-motion-safe audio design
- never make critical warnings audio-only
- subtitle/caption support must exist if voiced or speech-like content expands later

## Minimum slice audio checklist

- title sound identity
- menu confirm / cancel / invalid
- player attack and dodge
- enemy attack telegraph and hit
- player damage taken
- enemy defeat
- reward pickup
- reward confirm
- clear / fail stingers
- one floor loop and one boss loop

## Recommendation

Prototype fast with lightweight sources, but plan for professional audio polish before launch if the rebooted game proves worth expanding.
