# Vertical Slice Contract

Version date: April 14, 2026

This document defines the first rebuilt slice that must exist before broader content expansion resumes.

The source of truth above this file is still `C:/ddbd/dungeon_crawler_mobile_design_spec_codex.md`.

## Purpose

Build one short, replay-worthy, spec-compliant slice that proves the product is fun, readable, accessible, and recommendation-worthy on mobile before scaling content or polishing launch materials.

## What the slice must prove

1. A new player understands the fantasy quickly.
2. The first live decision is obvious.
3. The first fight feels good and readable.
4. Rewards feel exciting and mechanically clear.
5. The first run or floor ending creates a real urge to continue.
6. The post-run state answers `why play again right now?`

## Non-negotiables for the slice

- Accessibility is visible immediately from the opening flow.
- Required information is shown before the player acts.
- Selected-option consequences are visible on the same screen as the selected option.
- Rewards show exact before-and-after impact.
- Combat feedback shows exact health change and clearly explains why it happened.
- Companion choice explains why each companion is desirable over another option.
- The slice must feel playable, not like a tutorialized reading exercise.
- The product must not rely on fake depth, placeholder-feeling copy, or hidden state to seem interesting.

## Scope of the slice

Recommended target:

- opening screen
- onboarding
- class select
- companion select
- first meaningful floor
- one readable fight
- one meaningful reward or event
- one post-run / post-floor motivation screen

This is intentionally smaller than the full game, but it must feel like the real product rather than a mockup.

## What the slice must not do

- It must not treat one 10-floor clear as enough product value proof by itself.
- It must not rely on excessive text to carry the emotional weight.
- It must not allow players to bypass the core challenge and still feel like they fully solved the loop.
- It must not ask players to remember hidden values from earlier screens.

## Success checks

The slice is ready for outside testing when all of these are true:

- a tester can explain who they are and what they are trying to do
- a tester can identify danger, reward, and next action quickly
- a tester can explain why they picked a companion
- a tester can explain what a reward changed
- a tester can explain why they would or would not run again

## Expansion rule

Do not expand content breadth, pricing confidence, or launch confidence until this slice feels strong enough that outside testers would recommend it on its own merits.
