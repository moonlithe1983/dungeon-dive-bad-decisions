# Dungeon Dive: Bad Decisions Dialogue Style Guide

## Purpose

This guide keeps future writing aligned with the live product shape:

- title
- interactive onboarding tutorial
- class-select assigned-role briefing
- companion-select
- run-map
- battle / reward / event
- end-run recap / codex / progression

It exists to stop writing drift. New copy should make the loop sharper, not heavier.

## Core Rule

Priority order:

1. gameplay-facing reactive copy
2. recurring loop dialogue
3. codex and archive texture
4. background lore expansion

If a new line does not improve a live gameplay surface, it is probably not the next line to write.

## Story Delivery Rule

Story should enter through these surfaces first:

- onboarding tutorial
- route choice
- battle intros
- reward choices
- event choices
- companion reactions
- defeat recaps

New lore should only be added when it sharpens one of those surfaces.

## Voice Goals

- fast
- hostile-funny
- procedural horror
- survival-first
- readable on a phone in a few seconds

The tone should feel like corporate systems have become physically aggressive and nobody with power intends to help.

## Voice Constraints

- Keep lines short enough to scan during play.
- Prefer one idea per line.
- Put the actionable truth first.
- Let systems language carry the joke when possible.
- Avoid front-loaded exposition.
- Avoid generic fantasy diction.
- Avoid long speeches unless the moment is a boss, ending, or archive reveal.
- Do not imitate any specific existing franchise voice directly.

## Writing Hierarchy By Surface

### Onboarding

- Teach the player what the next action means.
- Sell the world through pressure, not lore dumps.
- Keep every line pointed at the next button press.

### Route Choice

- Clarify risk.
- Clarify temptation.
- Suggest what kind of person would choose this badly.

### Battle

- Make danger legible fast.
- Give classes and companions recognizable recurring cadence.
- Reward near-death or high-synergy moments with memorable short lines.

### Reward

- Make the choice feel like a build decision, not a generic loot pick.
- Let the language imply consequence, greed, or relief.

### Event

- Present a sharp premise fast.
- Make each option feel morally or strategically distinct.
- Never bury the actionable difference under lore.

### Defeat Recap

- Preserve dignity without draining the bite.
- Explain why the run failed in-world.
- Leave the player with one clear next-run instinct.

## Companion Writing Rule

Companion lines should mainly do one of four jobs:

- clarify risk
- intensify tone
- validate or challenge a choice
- make failure memorable

Companions are not the first framing device anymore. Class identity and the onboarding incident framing come first. Companion chemistry should reinforce the loop, not replace its structure.

## Do / Don't

### Route Choice

Do:

- "That hallway is smiling too hard. We go there, it wants something back."
- "Left path looks safer. Safer is how this place fattens you up."

Don't:

- "Long ago this corridor was used by Meridian's regional training teams during the second expansion wave."

### Low-Health Bark

Do:

- "You are one polite email away from dying in a hallway."
- "No more heroics. We are in paperwork-with-blood territory now."

Don't:

- "Your vitality is critically low and this is concerning from a narrative standpoint."

### Reward Pick

Do:

- "That one solves a problem. The other one creates an interesting lawsuit."
- "Take the ugly tool. Pretty tools got us into the tower."

Don't:

- "This relic was first developed by a subsidiary during a merger event..."

### Event Choice

Do:

- "One option costs blood. One costs dignity. Read carefully."
- "That machine is offering relief with terms attached. It thinks we're desperate."

Don't:

- "This scene symbolizes the tension between labor and executive control in the post-Everrise era."

### Defeat Recap

Do:

- "You died trying to stabilize a system that wanted witnesses more than survivors."
- "The run broke where you got greedy, tired, and slightly too confident. Good. That's usable."

Don't:

- "The protagonist's arc remains incomplete due to an unsuccessful combat outcome."

## Recurrence Targets

Players should be able to feel repeated class and companion identity across these triggers:

- tutorial start
- first route pick
- bad route pick
- low health
- elite warning
- perfect-synergy reward
- ugly event choice
- defeat recap
- win/extract recap

If the line would only be interesting once in a codex entry, it is probably the wrong priority for the next writing pass.
